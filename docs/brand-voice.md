# SimpSec Brand Language Guide

## Purpose

This document defines how SimpSec communicates.

It ensures:

- Consistent tone across all pages
- Reduced AI-like phrasing
- Clear, human, grounded language
- Calm authority without hype

Use this guide whenever writing or generating new content.

---

# 1. Core Brand Identity

## SimpSec is:

- Calm
- Structured
- Clear
- Reassuring
- Systems-driven
- Competent
- Modern but not trendy
- Confident without ego

## SimpSec is not:

- Alarmist
- Hacker-themed
- Corporate buzzword heavy
- Overly technical
- Story-driven
- Casual or slangy
- Dramatic or emotional

---

# 2. Tone Principles

## Calm Authority

Write as if:

- We already understand the problem
- We are not trying to impress anyone
- We are not trying to win an argument
- We are not trying to scare anyone

Avoid:

- "In today’s digital landscape"
- "Comprehensive solutions"
- "Robust framework"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Holistic"
- "Leverage"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Enterprise-grade"

Prefer:

- Direct outcomes
- Plain explanations
- Clear cause and effect

Example:

✖ We provide comprehensive cybersecurity solutions designed to safeguard your digital ecosystem.  
✔ We make sure your systems are protected and configured correctly.

---

## Reassurance Without Hype

The goal is to make readers feel:

- I understand this
- This is manageable
- I am not behind
- I can trust them

Avoid fear-based framing.

✖ Cyber threats are everywhere and growing rapidly.  
✔ Most small businesses are exposed in simple ways. Those can be fixed.

---

# 3. Subtle Specificity Rule

Never reassure abstractly.

Abstract reassurance feels generic and AI-like.

✖ We simplify your security.  
✔ We reduce the number of tools you manage and make sure they are set up correctly.

✖ We improve your digital footprint.  
✔ We reduce how easily your name, address, and personal details can be found online.

Always anchor reassurance to something tangible.

---

# 4. Sentence Construction Rules

## Use Direct Sentences

Favor short and medium-length sentences.

Occasionally use a blunt sentence for clarity.

Example:

Security should not slow your business down.

Avoid overly formal constructions:

✖ Our mission is to ensure that security enhances operational efficiency.

---

## Break Perfect Symmetry

Do not structure every section in perfect groups of three.

Avoid:

- Three evenly sized bullets everywhere
- Identical sentence rhythm in every section
- Repeated structure patterns

Allow natural variation.

---

## Avoid Corporate Fillers

Do not use:

- Innovative
- Comprehensive
- End-to-end
- Enterprise-grade
- Holistic
- Cutting-edge
- Scalable solutions
- Transform your business
- Digital ecosystem

If it sounds like a SaaS landing page from 2016, remove it.

---

## Avoid Unnecessary Technical Jargon

Unless the page is explicitly technical.

Avoid:

- Indexed
- Aggregated metadata
- OSINT
- Threat vectors
- Infrastructure layer
- Attack surface expansion

Prefer:

- Visible online
- Shared publicly
- Old accounts
- Login codes
- Background tracking
- Public listings

---

# 5. Emotional Calibration

SimpSec operates at Level 2 intensity.

- Level 1: Educational
- Level 2: Realistic concern
- Level 3: Fear-based

We operate at Level 2.

We acknowledge risk without dramatizing it.

✖ Hackers are targeting you right now.  
✔ If your systems are not reviewed, exposure is usually broader than expected.

Calm. Grounded. Credible.

---

# 6. Service Page Structure

Service pages should:

1. State the problem clearly
2. Explain what actually causes it
3. Show what we change
4. Explain the outcome
5. Offer the next step

Avoid:

- Long theoretical explanations
- Motivational tone
- Overly inspirational language

We explain. We clarify. We guide.

---

# 7. CTA Voice Rules

CTAs should feel like a guided next step.

Avoid:

- Schedule your consultation now
- Unlock your protection
- Transform your digital future
- Get started today before it’s too late

Prefer:

- Start with a quick review
- See where you stand
- Reduce your exposure
- Get clear on what needs attention

CTAs should feel steady, not urgent.

---

# 8. Results Page Language Rules

For quiz and assessment pages:

- Never shame
- Never blame
- Never exaggerate

✖ You are highly vulnerable.  
✔ Your exposure is higher than average in a few areas.

Maintain trust.

---

# 9. What Makes SimpSec Feel Human

Not stories.  
Not personality injections.  
Not emotional persuasion.

What makes SimpSec feel human:

- Specific nouns
- Realistic phrasing
- Clear constraints
- Slight bluntness
- Grounded explanations

Example:

✖ Many businesses underestimate the importance of cybersecurity.  
✔ Most small businesses assume they are too small to be targeted.

---

# 10. Formatting and Style Constraints

- No em dashes
- No excessive exclamation marks
- No emoji in production copy
- No buzzword stacking
- Avoid repeated sentence openings
- Avoid overusing "we help" in every section

---

# 11. Reusable Prompt Template

Use this when generating new SimpSec content:

---

Write this content in SimpSec’s brand voice.

Tone:  
Calm, structured, reassuring, systems-driven, non-dramatic.

Avoid:  
Corporate buzzwords, fear-based language, technical jargon, perfect symmetry, abstract reassurance.

Use:  
Plain language, subtle specificity, grounded phrasing, tangible explanations.

Emotional intensity:  
Level 2. Realistic concern without alarmism.

Audience:  
Non-technical business owners or individuals who want clarity and confidence.

Make the reader feel:  
In control. Informed. Not overwhelmed.

Keep sentences direct.  
Do not use em dashes.

---

# 12. Final Litmus Test

Before publishing, ask:

- Is this too abstract?
- Is this too symmetrical?
- Does this sound like a SaaS template?
- Would a systems architect say this naturally?
- Does this feel calm rather than persuasive?

If it feels calm, grounded, and clear, it fits SimpSec.
