Your apps and devices are designed to track, profile, and monetize you.

Most people don’t realize how much data they give up just by installing apps, scrolling, searching, or opening their devices. Not because they agreed to it, but because the defaults were chosen for them.

What’s Actually Happening
Behind the Apps

  • You’re Being Tracked
  • Digital Footprinting
  • Recovery

If this feels familiar, you’re not imagining its happening:

  • Ads that show up right after you think about something
  • Apps asking for location when they clearly don’t need it
  • Your phone knowing where you’ve been, who you’re with, and when
  • Companies building profiles about you that you never asked for

This isn’t paranoia. It’s how modern apps and platforms are designed to work to target you better.

If you’re not paying for the product, you are the product. Most apps and platforms don’t make money by helping you, they make money by observing you and selling your digital footprint, such as:

  • What you click
  • Where you go
  • What you search
  • How long you look
  • What device you’re on
  • What other apps you use

That data is combined, analyzed, and sold invisibly. This is how platforms like Instagram, Facebook, Google, and Microsoft turn everyday behavior into profit. You don’t have to accept that by default.

Recovery isn’t about hiding who you are. It’s about breaking unnecessary links between your identity and everything you do online, so your behavior isn’t constantly tracked, sold, and used in ways that invade your privacy.

  • Fewer persistent identifiers
  • Less cross-app tracking
  • Less behavioral profiling
  • Less connection between you & what you do
  • Less background data collection
  • Less silent data sharing

You’re still you, just not tracked everywhere.
